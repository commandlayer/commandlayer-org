# ERC-8004 and x402 compatibility

CommandLayer is not trying to replace every adjacent standard.

Its role is narrower: define the contract that agent systems execute and verify.

## How to read the relationship

- **CommandLayer** defines request and receipt meaning.
- **Agent Cards / ERC-8004-adjacent surfaces** help with discovery and registry patterns.
- **x402-adjacent surfaces** help with payment and transport patterns.

## Why compatibility matters

A contract layer is more useful when it can travel through existing rails.

That means CommandLayer receipts and requests should be understandable as the payload being discovered, transported, or settled — not as a competing replacement for every surrounding system.

## Practical takeaway

When explaining the stack:

- say that CommandLayer defines the contract
- say that discovery and payment rails can carry or reference that contract
- avoid implying that CommandLayer collapses discovery, payment, and execution into one undifferentiated product

## Asymmetry note

Commons is the cleaner canonical model. Commercial currently keeps a compatibility-preserving commerce shape for real payment and fulfillment surfaces. That is a product-shape decision, not a second theory of the stack.
