Sits on top of x402 + ERC-8004

CommandLayer is the semantic verb & schema layer for agents.

x402 moves money. ERC-8004 helps you find agents. CommandLayer defines what they do. A small, stable set of Commons verbs you can use today, the first Commercial verbs for economic flows (authorize, checkout, purchase, ship, verify), and Agent Cards bound to ENS and x402 so agents can advertise capabilities in a shared language.

What you can do right now

Use CommandLayer as the language contract between clients and agents — the part that standardizes the “exit names” and traffic rules on top of x402 and ERC-8004:

  • Pick a Commons verb (e.g. summarize).
  • Validate payloads against the pinned schemas.
  • Optionally describe your agent with an Agent Card.
  • Route through x402 / ERC-8004 compatible rails as they become available.

The three pieces of the semantic layer

You don’t need the whole stack to start. One verb + its schemas is enough. As x402 and ERC-8004 roll out, the same vocabulary scales across more agents, networks, and payment rails.

Commons

10 universal verbs for analysis, retrieval, summarization, and formatting. Stable, MIT-licensed, and pinned as the baseline command language.

View Commons verbs →

Commercial

Economic verbs for moving value: authorize, checkout, purchase, ship, and verify. Implemented as a separate tier that builds on Commons semantics and x402 transport.

Agent Cards

JSON descriptors that tie ENS names to verbs, schema versions, and x402 entrypoints so registries and clients can discover and route to your agent.
