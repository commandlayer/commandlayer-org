1. Execute

2. Emit receipt

3. Verify

One system, clear roles

Commons defines the contract, Runtime produces proof, Agent Cards identify agents, and Commercial extends the same model for paid flows.

Why it matters

If agents can spend money, call APIs, or move state, their actions need receipts.

Logs say something happened. A signed receipt says what executed, who signed it, and what contract shaped the result. That is the trust layer a skeptical operator, judge, or integration partner can inspect.

What a judge should understand fast

After a 20-second scan, the model should be simple:

  • agents execute actions
  • CommandLayer emits signed receipts
  • receipts can be verified independently
  • supporting layers keep that proof portable
