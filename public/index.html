Sits on top of x402 + ERC-8004

CommandLayer is the semantic verb & schema layer for agents.

Agents speak. x402 moves money. ERC-8004 helps you find agents. CommandLayer defines what they do — in a versioned, enforceable language.

  • A small, stable set of Commons verbs you can drop in today.
  • The first Commercial verbs for economic flows (authorize, checkout, purchase, ship, verify).
  • A growing Manifest of reserved future verbs for the agent economy.
  • Agent Cards bound to ENS and x402 so agents can advertise capabilities in a shared language.

Schemas and Agent-Cards are always free — versioned, immutable, and IPFS-mirrorable. The economics live in execution: Commons executes free on CommandLayer reference runtimes; Commercial execution is taxed at runtime. Every verb’s request/receipt schema is pinned to IPFS, checksummed, and anchored to its canonical ENS name.

Execution & Economic Model

Make meaning free. Make identity free. Make discovery free. Enforce truth with schemas and receipts. Then tax commercial execution when money moves.

  • Always free: schemas (Commons + Commercial) and Agent-Cards — versioned, immutable, IPFS-mirrorable.
  • Commons: free execution (best-effort reference runtime) + canonical request/receipt validation.
  • Commercial: schemas remain free; execution is taxed for throughput, guarantees, routing, and SLA-grade behavior.

What you can do right now

CommandLayer is the language contract between clients and agents — the part that standardizes verbs, request shapes, and receipts on top of x402. Schemas + Agent-Cards are free; interoperability comes from validation and receipts.

  1. Pick a Commons verb (e.g. summarize) and validate requests against the canonical schema.
  2. Publish an Agent Card so clients can discover and route to your agent by ENS.
  3. Use the same request/receipt contracts across runtimes; only execution changes.
  4. When you move to Commercial verbs, schemas stay free — and execution becomes taxable by design.

How the semantic layer is structured

You don’t need everything to start. One verb + its schemas is enough. Commons execution is free; Commercial execution is taxed — the contracts stay identical. As x402 and ERC-8004 roll out, the same vocabulary stretches across more agents, networks, and payment rails without changing request or receipt shapes. Schemas and Agent-Cards are pinned to IPFS with published checksums and bound to canonical ENS verbs, so the language stays stable even as runtimes and economics evolve. CommandLayer doesn’t compete with x402 or ERC-8004 — it completes the stack by defining machine intent.
