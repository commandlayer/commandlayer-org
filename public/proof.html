What each step proves

  • Resolve: signer identity and key material can be located for verification.
  • Sign: runtime emits a signed receipt tied to canonical payload bytes.
  • Verify: hash and signature checks match the signed receipt.
  • Tamper + reject: payload mutation breaks proof and is rejected.

What this does not replace

Live proof does not replace webhook sender authentication, payment-rail guarantees, or settlement systems. It proves the normalized event/action receipt and whether it verifies cryptographically.
