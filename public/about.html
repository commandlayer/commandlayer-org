About CommandLayer

Agents can act. CommandLayer proves they acted.

CommandLayer exists because agent execution needs evidence, not just logs. The system stays intentionally small: canonical contracts, signed receipts, verifiable identity, and execution surfaces that can be inspected instead of merely trusted.

What CommandLayer actually is

A trust layer for autonomous agent execution. The system is split on purpose so meaning stays portable and proof stays inspectable.

  • Commons: the canonical open contract layer and minimum verifiable receipt.
  • Commercial: a commerce-oriented extension built on the same contract discipline.
  • Agent Cards: discovery and routing metadata tied to identity.
  • Runtime: the proving surface that executes contracts and can attach execution metadata.

Open contracts, paid execution

CommandLayer keeps the contract layer open and puts monetization at execution. That is the business boundary and the trust boundary.

Protocol-Commons

The canonical public contract layer. Small, stable, and free to implement.

  • Schemas: FREE
  • Agent-Cards: FREE
  • Discovery (ENS): FREE
  • Reference runtime execution: available as a proving surface
Commons exists to: - define the minimum receipt - keep semantics portable - give builders one canonical starting point - make verification boring

Protocol-Commercial

A commerce-oriented extension that keeps schemas public while charging at execution time when risk, settlement, or fulfillment matter.

  • Schemas: FREE
  • Agent-Cards: FREE
  • Discovery: FREE
  • Managed execution: PAID when commerce-oriented guarantees matter
Commercial value accrues in: - execution of commercial verbs - throughput and routing - guarantees and certified delivery - settlement and fulfillment operations - managed service quality

Commons is the canonical open model. Commercial extends it without taking over product identity or closing the contract layer.

Where monetization lives

Monetization is not embedded in standards. It lives at execution time inside runtimes, where routing, guarantees, and operational responsibility already live.

Not in the contract

The schema, Agent Cards, ENS identity, or receipt verification. Meaning stays neutral and portable.

In execution

Execution: routing, metering, guarantees, fulfillment operations, and throughput control.

Why this matters

It keeps the story legible: open semantics, inspectable verification, and monetization where operational responsibility actually lives.

Execution model

No semantic fork. No paywalled contracts. Execution policy and guarantees live above the contract layer.

Runtime resolves ENS + Agent-Card Runtime identifies verb class: commons → free execution commercial → metered execution Runtime enforces: pricing quotas receipts that reflect commercial execution

CommandLayer keeps meaning open and monetizes execution where operational cost is real.

Why this architecture works

The architecture works because it keeps the contract layer portable while letting execution quality become the competitive surface.

  • You avoid paywalled standards.
  • You avoid “open core” backlash.
  • You align incentives with real value.
  • You monetize where enforcement is natural.
  • Semantics remain neutral — interoperability doesn’t get held hostage.

This keeps the protocol story and the business story aligned.
