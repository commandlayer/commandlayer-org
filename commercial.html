What the Commercial layer is for

Commercial verbs capture the high-value actions agents perform: taking payments, settling purchases, shipping goods, scheduling services, verifying identities, and enforcing authorization. Where Commons is universal, Commercial is explicitly economic.

Economic primitives

Verbs that map directly to real-world flows and revenue: purchases, settlement, shipping, identity checks, and more.

Paid integrations

These verbs are designed to plug into processors, wallets, and rails via x402, with clear room for premium or licensed use.

Composable with Commons

Commercial verbs assume Commons exists: analyze, summarize, parse, and format often wrap around economic actions.

Commercial verbs — registry (v1.0.0)

Initial set of economic verbs. Schemas are defined and versioned per verb, without touching Commons. Status indicates how mature each definition is.

Licensing & economics

Commercial verbs are where most long-term revenue will sit. They’re designed so operators, providers, and platforms can bolt on pricing, routing, and premium guarantees without breaking the open protocol.

The exact licensing model for Commercial verbs (usage tiers, premium integrations, etc.) can evolve over time, but the names and paths reserved here are stable for v1.0.0. Implementations can iterate behind the same schema contracts, with breaking changes moving to new versions.
